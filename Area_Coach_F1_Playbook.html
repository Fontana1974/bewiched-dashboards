For Rich, Ian & Jon, on a store visit. The F1 score tells you what's broken. This gets you to why — and the play to run before you leave.

The visit, in four moves

  1. Pull the F1 shape. Open the store's Op's Excellence tab. Note: queue average & Working-the-Queue (speed), Hello / Goodbye / How-are-you (hospitality), Total Score vs the ≤210 bench, latest finish — and who ran the audited shift. Sometimes it's one person to coach, not "the store".
  2. Read 1 — what's the gap? Speed, hospitality, or both.
  3. Read 2 — why? (30 seconds on the floor) Skill (they can't) · System (the setup's wrong for the bodies on shift) · State (they can, but the energy's off).
  4. Pick below, run the play, close the loop — one owner, one Big 5 anchor, one measure + a re-audit date.

Close the loop — every visit, no exceptions

"Speak to the manager" is not a fix. Leave with:

  • One owner — usually you (the Area Coach), sometimes the SM.
  • One Big 5 / value anchor — so the fix has language.
  • One measure + a re-audit date — Quali next week, or Race in two.
  • An escalation rule — coaching → development plan → seat change. Don't protect a seat for sentiment.
